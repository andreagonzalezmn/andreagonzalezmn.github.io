Frutis


Frutis is a service proposal that aims to reduce the waste of fruits and vegetables within the market called "Central de Abasto" in Mexico City through its management and distribution to generate conscious consumption among vendors and consumers.



← Past project Next project →
DURATION

March 2022 to March 2023 (1 year)


DESIGN TEAM

Sairi Ríos, Industrial Designer

RESPONSIBILITIES

• User Research (planning and execution of research studies, conducting interviews, surveys, guerrilla research, data collection and analysis, and generation of user insights)
• End-to-end user experience design (creation of journey maps, product and capital flowcharts, and visuals for a better understanding of the project)
• Analysis and optimization of workflows and identification of improvement opportunities
• User-centered decision making and drafting of design principles with a conscious, responsible and collaborative consumption approach

TOOLS
Miro Illustrator Photoshop Design Thinking Journey Map Survey Interview Double diamond User research
BRIEF

This project was carried out from the "Service Design Challenge 2022" contest, where it was requested to design a service of any type, as long as it was conscious, responsible and the collaborative consumption was taken into account, in addition to the redefinition of the concept of property through the circular economy. It is worth mentioning that this service design was taken as the thesis project for getting the bachelor's degree.

PLANNING

The tool used for the development of this project was the double diamond with the help of the Design Thinking methodology. To carry out the project in the shortest possible time but with effective results, a Gantt chart was made from the double diamond scheme mentioned above.

RESEARCH

Secondary research allowed us to find the issue of fruits and vegetables in the Central de Abasto as an option with more opportunity for improvement in Mexico, taking into account factors such as the accessibility of research that we had and the possibility of creating a viable and timeless service.

Subsequently, we began the planning and implementation of quantitative and qualitative research in the Central de Abasto to understand the motivations and needs of the actors or people involved in the consumption of fruits and vegetables. 12 vendors were interviewed within the Central de Abasto and 33 consumers from Mexico City were surveyed through Google Forms, in addition a guerrilla investigation of the vendors of the Central was carried out.


INSIGHTS

There are 4 essential actors in the management, distribution and consumption within the Central de Abasto:

• Wholesalers: They sell the product in large quantities and do not have much variety

• Retailers: They sell the product in small quantities and in variety

• Consumers: Internal food vendors or external people who buy products at the Central de Abasto

• Diableros: They help the internal transport of product

The greatest waste of fruit and vegetables occurs as a result of its mismanagement by wholesalers, because they throw away all the merchandise that is left over per day in some organic waste containers in the part of the production area regardless of whether it is still suitable for consumption or not.




The diableros as part of the solution

Although the diableros are not involved in product waste, they are aware of the need for change and a better use of them. These workers, who are dedicated to the transport of internal product, were taken into account for the implementation of the service in the final phase.

RESULTS AND ACHIEVEMENTS

As a result of the first 3 phases of the project, a small company called Frutis was created, which collects and sells fruits and vegetables that due to their aesthetics, size or surplus are discarded within the Central de Abasto.

How does it work

Through the collection of the aforementioned product with the help of diableros who are dedicated to collecting it and bringing it to our vendor trucks located in the loading and unloading area within the Central for subsequent sale.

Paradigm shift

How can we create a true paradigm shift focused on a conscious and circular economy? It is not only worrying about the correct use of fruits and vegetables, but also about people and creating a connection in which everyone feels proud of their work to the point that it is recognized and valued by outsiders, thus creating a healthy interaction between consumer-workers-product.

End-to-end design

Being very clear about the tasks and the importance of each one of the actors is essential for a service to succeed, since options must be generated that prevent possible risks that affect the quality of the service and therefore the customer experience.




The Service Blueprint and the Journey Map are tools that will help to take into account all the possible scenarios within the designed service, each actor involved and the customer experience throughout the purchase journey, in addition to contemplating what could happen in the event of some mishap and how can you act in this situation.

Next steps

Carrying out a business plan is a complex issue that requires money and time, which is why we define key aspects for its future implementation, as well as the general analysis of the company that serves as an initial structure to be able to build on it, increase the chances of success and in the future get a good positioning of the brand through the formation of strategic alliances.


CHALLENGES AND LEARNINGS
The complexity of the topic

Conscious, responsible and collaborative consumption, together with the circular economy, are complex concepts that encompass various economic, social and environmental aspects, so fully understanding them required extensive research and a deep understanding of multiple disciplines.

Identification of opportunities and design of innovative solutions

The thesis involved analyzing the needs and motivations of users, as well as the current barriers and challenges that arise in the country in the field of conscious consumption. Identifying relevant and viable opportunities required a creative approach and critical analysis.

Evaluation and impact measurement

Carrying out a business plan is a complex issue, which is why we define key aspects for its future implementation, as well as the general analysis of the company, which serves as an initial structure to be able to build on it, thus increasing the possibilities of success and have a good positioning of the brand through the formation of strategic alliances to publicize Frutis and its objective.

Sustainability and ethics

We had the opportunity to gain a deep understanding of the environmental, social, and ethical challenges related to consumption and the economy in Mexico.
