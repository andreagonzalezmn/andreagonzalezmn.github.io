Kosmo


Kosmo is a meditation solution for anyone who wants to start or continue their path towards guided meditation and has little time for it.

In kosmo there is no obligation, you can meditate when you think it is necessary and you want to give peace to your mind and soul to continue with daily life, for this reason it contains quick meditations to listen to on the way to work, school or when you have free time. Kosmo also has courses focused on certain skills and contexts, which are made up of online lessons where you can practice with experts in the field.

DURATION

April 9 to 15, 2023 (1 week)

RESPONSIBILITIES

This project was entirely my responsibility and I worked individually for the purpose of practicing skills focused on UX/UI design.

TOOLS
Figma Illustrator Photoshop Premiere Pro After Effects Double diamond Journey Map Research plan Interview What if...? How might we Wireframing Nielsen Usability Analysis
BRIEF

Create a meditation app from scratch to practice research skills, user experience, Nielsen usability heuristic analysis and visual design.

PLANNING

For this project I used the double diamond methodology to structure and visualize my creative process, dividing each phase and organizing my work days in order to complete this project in one week.

RESEARCH

I carried out primary and secondary research to obtain qualitative and quantitative data about the meditation meaning, the ways to do it and its benefits.

The main technique used in the primary research phase was the interview, which was carried out with 15 users between 20 and 30 years of age with the aim of:

• Know their perspective on meditation
• To know if they have had interaction with meditation and in what way they practice it or would be willing to practice it

Likewise, these data gave guidelines for the contribution to the project through:

• The knowledge of the basic and convenient needs for the users and at the same time for the characteristics of the app
• User persona specification


INSIGHTS

Thanks to secondary research with the search for statistics, analogues and homologues and primary research from interviews, I determined that meditation is rarely practiced by people even though they have an idea of the benefits it can bring to their lives. In addition, these people live with a lack of time to relax and get to know themselves.

Another discovery was that, during the practice of meditation, some people stop carrying it out due to ignorance of what kind of technique to perform and out of despair at not obtaining the expected results in a short time.

Target

People between 20 and 30 years old who are interested in meditating but do not do so due to lack of time and knowledge of it.


Context

Users who are affected by the stress of everyday life and who want to improve their lifestyle, their decision making and their emotional health.

RESULTS AND ACHIEVEMENTS

A high-fidelity prototype was completed in the incredibly short period of just one week, after which some usability testing was done with users who helped to iterate the process and improve some UI aspects.

Despite not having been a project released to the market, this helped me to practice my acquired knowledge regarding user-centered design, service design and unify them to further enrich and practice in UI/UX design.


Concept

The cosmos denotes order and organization, as we want our day to day to be, as well as representing the universe (the set of everything in our life) as an ordered or harmonious system (this system is mind and soul).



Splash screens

These welcome screens play a crucial role in the initial impression that users have of the application, they can influence their decision to continue using it or to abandon it.



Meditation and motivation at the reach of a click

Quick, clear, simple: The home screen offers an option of textual quotes so that the user can read them at any time in case they need a little motivation without having to listen to audios.




Meditation courses

To delve deeper into a problem or skill, the app offers complete courses to improve and reinforce certain aspects that the user wants to focus on.




Quick meditation tracks are meditation audios of less than 10 minutes so that the user can listen to them quickly.

CHALLENGES AND LEARNINGS
Time and resource management

Working individually meant taking on multiple roles, as well as improving my decision-making and technical skills. For this I had to carefully plan my tasks, set priorities and balance the time spent on research, design and testing.

Design and user experience

Design and user experience are critical elements for the success of an application. Creating an intuitive, attractive and easy-to-use interface was a challenge due to my lack of experience in the field of mobile digital products, which is why I had to make sure I understood the needs of my users using research tools such as the Journey Map, What if?, conducting interviews and prototyping in low and high fidelity.
