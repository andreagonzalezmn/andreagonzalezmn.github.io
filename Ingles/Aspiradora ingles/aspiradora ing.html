Robot Vacuum
Air V-Zero


The objective of this school project was to learn the system "man - object - cultural environment" focusing on the factors of production, function, ergonomics and mainly through the analysis of a brand to design the end-to-end of a product, in this case a robot vacuum.



← Past project Next project →
DURATION

September to November 2021 (3 months)


DESIGN TEAM

Sairi Ríos, Industrial Designer
Lizzette Labastida, Industrial Designer

RESPONSIBILITIES

• Market research and user analysis
• Generation of ideas and development of design concepts: Sketches, 3D modeling, renderings, animations and graphic design
• Ergonomics definition: Product dimensions, shapes, functions, materials and textures
• Reverse engineering, definition of quality and safety standards according to ISO regulations
• User interface design according to the Nielsen usability heuristic analysis: Control panels, buttons, screens and other interactive elements
• Documentation, preparation of technical plans and executive presentations

TOOLS
Rhinoceros 6 Keyshot 10 Figma Illustrator Photoshop Premiere Pro Design Thinking Journey Map Survey Wireframing
BRIEF

What would happen if Nike designed a robot vacuum cleaner?

The exercise consisted of designing a family of products for the Nike brand:

A robot vacuum cleaner and its charging base, which are completely different products, since the Nike brand is sporty. The factors that we had learned in the previous semesters (production, function and ergonomics) also had to be taken into account, but with a focus on the aesthetics of the product.

PLANNING

The tool used for the development of this project was Design Thinking, that is made up of 4 stages which are considered iterative depending on the needs in each of them.

Due to the complexity of this exercise and the little time we had to do it, we decided to make a Gantt chart to measure the times, hold online meetings outside of the class and distribute the tasks equally in the team.

RESEARCH AND PROCESS

From existing products, social networks, advertising, website, applications, commercials and surveys, information was collected where determining factors were found to be able to transmit a concept according to the identity of the brand.

Based on market research, ideas and design concepts were generated for the robot vacuum development with the help of initial sketches, renderings, and prototypes that represented the appearance and functions of the product.

Because Nike does not specialize in home appliance technologies, we chose the Dyson brand as a technology partner to provide the necessary technology and production services.

To really understand and find out how it works and how the product can be improved, a real robot vacuum was taken apart. This allowed us to list and identify each component so we could start generating ideas through sketches and modeling:


RESULTS AND ACHIEVEMENTS





Nike Air

We use Nike Air for inspiration as it is one of the main brand segments due to the versatility of the design with innovative shapes and technologies. In an analysis, we found some relevant points such as solids with curves that generate visual acuity and dynamism through curves with tensions, fluctuations and organic factors such as high and low relief.



Made to size

The general volume is a surface built by two rails that create a double curvature and a circular top view with curves that give rhythm.

Its side view was designed from segmented curves to give a dynamic overall shape. It has a height of 3.9 in and a diameter of 11.8 in, so the diameter is three times the height.



Full control through the app

For the correct use of the vacuum cleaner it is necessary to: Remove any obstacle from the floor.

To turn it on, you need to press the On/Off button and open the app to start the pairing, then a notification will be displayed through the control panel of the product and the app.

If there is a problem, the app will give indications of the solution and the robot will automatically recharge and continue to fill its battery. It is necessary to check when the garbage container is full in order to dispose of the garbage.


Extraordinarily powerful

The product weighs 6.6 pounds, its charging time is 165 minutes, and its solid waste containment capacity is 12.8 oz. Also, simultaneous intelligent mapping and location technology allows the robot vacuum cleaner to know its position within the room.

Long-range sensors go up to 6.5 ft, short-range obstacle sensors see up to 1.9 ft and wall sensors up to 0.49 ft.



Precision from the inside out

The casings are made of ABS, silicone rubber and polycarbonate for their mechanical properties for household products. The buttons are made of ABS for its smooth texture to the touch. Dyson cyclones, on the other hand, give you the most debris suction as it creates a linear path of airflow.


CHALLENGES AND LEARNINGS
Integration of principles

The initial challenge lay in making decisions to find a balance and coherence between the principles of production, function, ergonomics and also giving priority to aesthetics.

Exhaustive investigation

This involved understanding the market, identifying user needs and desires, researching design trends, and thoroughly exploring and learning the capabilities, functionality, and technical requirements of each component. I learned that research gives a solid foundation to make informed decisions and create a relevant and engaging products.

Iterations and 3D prototyping

Designing from scratch requires an iterative mindset, so we had to run multiple iterations and prototypes to refine the design and ensure it adhered to established principles. The 3D prototyping and reverse engineering process of taking apart a robot vacuum gave us the opportunity to test and evaluate the product at different stages to make improvements and adjustments before final delivery.

Continuous learning and adaptation

The ability to work as a team on such a complex project and in an online mode due to the pandemic taught me the importance of adapting to changes, learning from mistakes and evolving my design language by using technical terms and learning to present the project.
